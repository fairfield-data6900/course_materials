### 💾 THE VISUAL GUIDE: SAVING YOUR WORK (THE COMMIT)

Follow these steps exactly to complete the "Commit" part of your **Verification Ritual**.

**1. Open the Source Control Sidebar**
*   Look at the **far left vertical activity bar** (where the icons are).
*   Find the icon that looks like **three circles connected by lines** (the "Branch" icon). 
*   *Tip: It is usually the 3rd icon from the top. When you hover over it, it says "Source Control."*
*   If you see a **blue circle with a number** on it, that means you have unsaved changes!

**2. Prepare Your Changes (Staging)**
*   In the sidebar that opens, you will see a list of files under **"Changes."**
*   Find your `spec.md` file.
*   Click the small **plus sign (+)** that appears next to the filename when you hover over it. 
*   *The file will move up to a new section called **"Staged Changes."** This means it is "packed and ready to go."*

**3. Label Your Work (The Message)**
*   Look for the empty box that says **"Message."**
*   Type exactly what you did in plain English.
*   *Example: "Drafted the Meeting Summary spec"*

**4. The First Save (Commit)**
*   Click the big blue button that says **Commit**.
*   *Wait a second. Your changes will disappear from the list. They aren't gone—they are now part of your project's history!*

**5. The Final Push (Sync Changes) — IMPORTANT!**
*   After you click Commit, the big blue button will change.
*   It will now say **Sync Changes** (with a small circular arrow icon 🔄).
*   **Click Sync Changes.** 
*   *A small popup might ask if you want to push and pull; click **OK**. Once the blue bar at the top stops moving, your work is officially on GitHub and safe.*
