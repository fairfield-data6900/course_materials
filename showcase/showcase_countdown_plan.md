## Dress Rehearsal (Week 7 class)

This is not a test — it's a learning opportunity. You present, peers score you on the rubric, you get honest feedback. The goal is to find out what needs fixing *before* the professionals show up.

**What to bring:**
- Working demo (Happy Path)
- Slides (even if rough)
- HANDOFF.md draft (peers will score documentation quality)
- A pen — you'll be taking notes on feedback

## The Audience Shift

This is the most important thing to internalize between the dress rehearsal and the showcase:

| Dress Rehearsal | Showcase |
|:----------------|:---------|
| Peers who know your project | Professionals seeing it for the first time |
| Feedback-oriented | Impression-oriented |
| "What should we improve?" | "Would I hire this team?" |
| Mistakes are learning moments | Mistakes are remembered |
| Jargon is understood | Jargon is confusing |
| 25 minutes feels long | 25 minutes flies by |

Everything you say, show, and hand over should answer one question from the audience's perspective: **"So what?"**

## Daily Standup (Recommended)

Every day, each team member answers three questions in your team channel (Slack, group chat, whatever you use). Takes 2 minutes. Keeps everyone honest.

1. **What did I finish yesterday?**
2. **What am I doing today?**
3. **Am I blocked on anything?**

PM: if someone is blocked two days in a row, escalate to office hours.
